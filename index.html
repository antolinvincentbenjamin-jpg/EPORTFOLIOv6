My Family

I was born on July 27, 2011 to a family comprising of 8 members. I'm beyond grateful to the Lord for giving me the chance to grow up with a complete and loving family. My family composed of my mom Rose, my dad Vincent, my ate Vivien, my grandfather Benjamin, my grandmother Nenita, my tita Janeth, and my tita Roan. They've all shown me the true meaning of unconditional love because no matter how many times I make a mistake, no matter how many times I fall or sin, they keep on cheering me on as if nothing ever happened.

My father, who has a profession in Computer Engineering, is one of the most hardworking people I've ever encountered. He works six days out of the week starting from 8 AM in the morning all the way to 7 PM at night. Even though he works and works all day, he never misses a chance to check up on my mom and ask how she's doing. When he gets home from work he almost always has a pasalubong for me, my ate, and of course my mom. He is one of the most loving people I've met as well, even though he may appear not too caring because of work. Most of the things I have right now, my computer to which I found out costed more than 100k, my tablet, most of my toys over the years, my piano, my shoes, my clothes, and a lot more, have come from him. I don't know where he gets the money, I don't know why he buys so many things for me even though I don't ask for them. I am really grateful for all of it though because I know other people, other children, don't get treated the same way I do. I know I'm privileged, maybe too privileged because sometimes I catch myself slipping into being spoiled. Even though me and my dad have had our disagreements here and there, especially when it came to me going to the mall with my friends, we still manage to fix our problems together. He told me before that fixing issues together is the greatest form of bonding.

My mother, who has a profession in the City Government, is no doubt the hardest working person I know. She works the entire week because even when she's home she still has to finish writing and signing documents, day and night. When I wake up at 3 AM she's already awake, when I sleep at 11 PM she's still awake. I don't even know if she sleeps anymore. Amidst all this she still manages to cook me breakfast, lunch, and dinner. She still washes the dishes, washes our clothes, cleans the house, and a lot more. Of course I feel bad for my mom and I'm mad at myself because I'm so lazy to the point I don't even help her. However, now when I wake up I cook the rice, I boil the water, sometimes I wash the dishes. There are times when I don't eat my lunch and I'm reminded that she worked so hard to prepare me a homecooked meal with love, I feel bad really bad. She doesn't buy herself anything yet she buys me a lot of things, gives me my allowance, buys me food. She is the most supportive person I know because even when she's not present in my competitions she still cheers me on online or before I leave the house. I feel ashamed to be their child, embarrassed actually, because they are so hardworking, so loving and so patient, yet here I am the laziest person ever who constantly tries to push them away.

Sometimes I feel unworthy to be in this family because I'm the only one who can't do anything good, especially now that I'm drowning in failure academically and physically. My body is in constant need of medication because of my anemia and frail immune system and I feel like I'm a burden on them because my medication is so expensive and I don't even have a salary so they're forced to carry the weight of my problems. I remember last year during Christmas break when we went to Spain to visit our relatives for the holidays, I really wanted to give up because at that time I was really and absolutely drowning in missed activities because of my hospital visits, I was convinced my grades were doomed. I wrote a prayer on a piece of paper asking God to just let me pass away already, I accepted death with open arms, but then my parents found the note even though I thought I've disposed of it already. They reminded me of their love, of my worth, and even if my grades fail, even if I don't accomplish all the things I want, as long as I am still who I am, they will always be proud of me no matter what. Honestly, after that I felt even more unworthy of being in such a loving family. I don't know what I did in the past life to deserve this, this is way more than what I certainly deserve.

My ate who doesn't even have a job yet gives me an allowance as well, buys me things, and supports my every decision as long as it doesn't hurt anybody especially myself. Though we've also had disagreements in the past, we've managed to put all of those aside. She helps me with some of my assignment even though I don't ask her to because I feel ashamed to say that I can't do it. She takes me out to go eat, to go watch a movie, or just to go to a park or someplace. I'm really thankful because she shows so much love to me even though I've never equally reciprocated that love.

My grandmother, who was once a City Councilor in Batangas, was the most loving person I ever met, she passed away 5 years ago. When she was alive, she was the one who cooked our meals even at her old age. She taught me values like resilience, and kindess. Suddenly nobody was there to hear my cries, my pleas, and my lament. It's not that my parents don't want to listen to me, it's that I insist on being fine because I don't want to add to their burden.

My grandfather, who was a City Councilor in LPC and now currently a kagawad in Pulanglupa Uno, also taught me a lot of values, a lot of lessons, a lot of skills. Though he lectures me here and there, those scoldings have more love than a simple "I love you"

Both my titas are also hardworking people. They're both not married because they chose to pursue their careers in teaching and government respectively so that they can give their money and efforts to our family. They chose family over themselves which breaks my heart knowing they had their own dreams and passions supressed just to keep this family afloat one time.

I'm guessing it's pretty evident that my family is very much involved in government, especially my grandparents, granduncles and grandaunts. Welcome to the world of Economics

Lesson 1: Demand

Short Discussion: Ang demand ay tumutukoy sa dami ng produkto o serbisyo na nais at kayang bilhin ng mga konsyumer sa isang takdang panahon at presyo. Kasama rito ang dalawang mahalagang elemento: ang kagustuhan (wants) at kakayahan (ability) ng mamimili. Makikita rin dito ang ugnayan ng presyo at dami, kapag mas mababa ang presyo, kadalasang mas mataas ang quantity demanded, at kapag tumaas ang presyo, bumababa ang demand (ceteris paribus). Maraming salik ang nakakaapekto sa demand gaya ng kita ng mamimili, panlasa, presyo ng mga kahalili, inaasahang pagbabago sa presyo, at populasyon. Mayroon ding tinatawag na demand schedule, ito ay isang talaan ng mga pagbabago sa quantity demand at presyo. Meron ding demand function, ito ay ang Qd = a = bP, at ito ay kalaunang mairerepresenta bilang isang Demand Curve.

Personal Reflection: Natutunan ko na ang bawat desisyon ko sa pamimili tulad ng pagpili ng murang produkto o paghihintay ng sale at discount ay may epekto sa ekonomiya. Napagtanto ko na kung marami kaming kailangang bilhin sa bahay, dapat kaming magplano at huwag basta-basta bumili. Kung ipapaliwanag ko ito sa kaklase, sasabihin ko na ang demand ay parang listahan ng gusto mong bilhin batay sa pera mo at kung gaano kataas ang presyo. Mahalaga ito dahil kapag naiintindihan natin ang demand, mas magiging matalino tayo sa paggastos at makakatulong sa sarili natin at pamilya.

Lesson 2: Supply

Short Discussion: Ang supply naman ay tumutukoy sa dami ng produkto o serbisyo na handang ipagbili o iprovide ng mga prodyuser sa iba't ibang presyo at takdang panahon. Karaniwang mas mataas ang supply kapag tumataas ang presyo dahil hinihikayat nito ang mga prodyuser na gumawa ng mas marami, at bumababa ito kapag mababa ang presyo o mataas ang gastos sa produksyon. Kasama sa salik na nakaaapekto sa supply ang teknolohiya, presyo ng input, bilang ng prodyuser, inaasahang pagbabago sa presyo, at iba pa. Meron ding itong supply schedule na katulad lang ng demand scedule, supply function na katulad lang din ng demand function, at supply curve na isang graph din.

Personal Reflection: Sa pag-aaral ng supply, natutunan ko na hindi lang sa mamimili umiikot ang ekonomiya, may malaking bahagi rin ang mga nagbebenta at ang kanilang kakayahang magbenta. Napagtanto ko na kapag maraming supply, mas may pagpipilian ang mamimili at mas bumababa ang presyo. Kung ipapaliwanag ko ito sa kaklase, sasabihin ko na para kang nagbebenta ng mga gawa mo, kapag mabenta ang gawa mo sa mas mataas na presyo, gagawa ka pa dahil sulit.

Lesson 3: Elasticity

Short Discussion: Ang elasticity o elastisidad ay tumutukoy sa antas ng pagtugon ng demand o suplay sa pagbabago ng presyo. Kapag elastik, malaki ang pagbabago ng demand kapag tumaas o bumaba ang presyo; kapag di-elastik, maliit lamang ang pagbabago dahil ito ay pangunahing pangangailangan. Mayroon ding ganap na elastik, ganap na di-elastik, at unitary elastic na nagpapakita ng ibaâ€™t ibang antas ng pagtugon sa presyo. Mahalaga ito sa ekonomiya upang maunawaan ng mga negosyante at pamahalaan kung paano gumagalaw ang pamilihan. Kapag higit sa 1, nangangahulugan itong Elastik, kapag mas mababa sa 1, ito ay di-elastik, 0 naman ay ganap na di-elastik, infinity ay ganap na elastik, at kung saktong 1 ito ay unitary.

Personal Reflection: Natutunan ko na ang elastisidad ay hindi lang tungkol sa presyo kundi sa ugali at pangangailangan ng tao. Sa tunay na buhay, may mga bagay tayong handang bilhin kahit mataas ang presyo, at may mga bagay na agad nating tinitigilan kapag mahal.

Short Discussion: Ang market equilibrium ay ang punto kung saan ang quantity demanded ay katumbas ng quantity supplied, dito nagkakasundo ang mamimili at prodyuser sa presyo at dami ng produkto. Sa puntong ito, walang sobra (surplus) o kakulangan (shortage). Kapag lumayo sa equilibrium na mas maraming supply kaysa sa demand, may sobrang produkto at bumababa ang presyo, kapag mas maraming demand kaysa supply, may kakulangan at tumataas ang presyo. Ito ang balanse ng pamilihan.

Personal Reflection: Sa araling ito, naunawaan ko na ang ekwilibriyo ng pamilihan ay tungkol sa balanse ng gusto ng tao at kaya ng gumawa. Napagtanto ko na sa buhay, hindi rin natin matatamo ang tamang resulta kung sobra o kulang ang ginagawa natin parang sa pamilihan lang./p>

Lesson 5: Estruktura ng Pamilihan

Short Discussion: Ang estruktura ng pamilihan ay tumutukoy sa uri ng kompetisyon at organisasyon ng pamilihan maaari itong perfect competition (ganap na kompetisyon), monopoly (monopolyo), monopsonyo, oligopoly (oligopolyo), o monopolistic competition (monopolistikong kompetisyon). Tinatalakay nito kung paano naapektuhan ng bilang ng prodyuser, uri ng produkto, at hadlang sa pagpasok sa merkado ang relasyon ng presyo at dami.

Personal Reflection: Dito ko nakitang hindi lahat ng produkto ay patas ang laban. Natuto akong maging mapanuri sa mga brand at tanungin kung bakit ganoon ang presyo at kalidad. Mahalaga ito sa pagsulong ng negosyo at pagiging patas ng merkado.

Lesson 6: Bahaging Ginagampanan ng Pamahalaan sa Regulasyon

Short Discussion: Ang pamahalaan ay may tungkuling pangasiwaan ang pamilihan upang mapanatili ang kaayusan at protektahan ang mamimili at prodyuser. Sa pamamagitan ng mga patakarang tulad ng price ceiling, price floor, buwis, subsidiya, at regulasyon ng kompetisyon, naiiwasan ang pananamantala at kawalang-katarungan sa kalakalan. Ginagawa ito ng pamahalaan upang masiguro ang balanseng ekonomiya at kapakinabangan ng lahat.

Personal Reflection: Nauunawaan kong ang pamahalaan ay parang tagapamagitan sa pagitan ng mamimili at prodyuser. Kung wala ito, posibleng maging magulo at hindi patas ang pamilihan. Mahalaga ang mga patakarang ito upang mapanatili ang disiplina sa ekonomiya.
